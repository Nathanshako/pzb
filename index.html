Why we started this

The story behind PZB

PZB started as a conversation between two friends, Dan Mwamba and Nathan Shako, about something they kept seeing: people chasing image while quietly letting their character slide. Followers, titles, and applause were treated like the brand itself — when really, those things are just the reflection.

They built PZB on a simpler idea. Your reputation isn't manufactured in public — it's earned in private, in the choices nobody applauds and nobody punishes. The brand you're protecting was never the logo. It's you.

Real moments

The standard in action

The pillars aren't slogans. They show up in small, unwatched decisions — here's what that actually looks like.

Respect

A junior teammate makes a mistake in front of the whole team. You correct it privately afterward, not publicly in the moment.

Integrity

You notice an invoice was overpaid in your favor. No one would've caught it. You flag it anyway.

Purpose

You turn down a deal that pays well but conflicts with what you actually stand for — because the money was never the point.

Legacy

You take time to mentor someone with nothing to gain, because someone once did the same for you.

Questions

Frequently asked

PZB — Protekt Ze Brand — is a standard for living, built around one idea: your reputation is your brand, and you protect it through your actions, not your image. It's a mindset and a community, not a product.

Anyone who wants to hold themselves to a higher personal standard — in business, relationships, or daily life. There's no application or qualification. You decide to live it.

It's a stylistic choice — part of the PZB identity, alongside the deliberate "Protekt" spelling. Together they form the acronym PZB and give the brand its own distinct voice.

Start by taking the PZB Badge of Vow above. Beyond that, follow PZB on social media for ongoing content, and reach out directly if you want to collaborate or bring PZB to your organization.

At its core, PZB is a standard people choose to live by. Get in touch if you'd like to know more about how Dan and Nathan are building it out.
