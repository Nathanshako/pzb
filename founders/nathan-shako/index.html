How PZB started

My name is Nathan Shako, co-founder of Protekt Ze Brand (PZB) and founder of Driflare. The journey of PZB started in 2025, when I joined Form 3 after performing well in the Junior Certificate Examinations (JCE). At that time, my classmate Dan Mwamba, who had a strong sense of vision and leadership, shared an idea that would later grow into something much bigger.

Dan noticed that as students moved through school, their behavior and reputation changed over time. He introduced the idea of “brand” as reputation — how others see your discipline, character, and conduct. The message “Protect your brand” quickly resonated with students and began to spread naturally.

When I first heard Dan explain it, I saw something more than a school message. I saw a concept that could grow beyond the classroom. In December 2025, I began refining the idea and helped shape it into the name Protekt Ze Brand, short form PZB.

“Protect your brand” became the seed of a larger vision about identity, discipline, and how people present themselves to the world.

From idea to vision

I helped create the first social media pages for PZB, shared the idea with Dan, and together we began shaping the early identity of the brand. We designed the first logo and later improved it as the concept matured.

As the idea grew, we explored possible futures for PZB, including fashion, music, and film. By May 2026, I was also using AI tools to help build a full website for PZB as part of my startup Driflare. We are still early, but our goal remains the same: to build PZB into a global identity centered on discipline, reputation, and creative expression.
